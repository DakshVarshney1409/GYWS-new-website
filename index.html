HOW WE STARTED

Mr. Mrinal Kanti Bhanja, or Dada as we call him, in his early twenties had the vision to give back to society by helping in uplifting the less fortunate. When he came across a few IITians who wanted to volunteer for a good cause but lacked the right platform- Gopali Youth Welfare Society came into being. Together, the Young Samaritans along with some Professors of IIT Kharagpur set on this journey and through the years GYWS has not just been instrumental in providing primary education to children at JVM, but has also undertaken different social initiatives to uplift the society! In Dada’s own words “ GYWS is aiding social, economic and cultural development of weaker sections of society while encouraging people to seek solutions to their everyday problems within locally available resources!”

MISSION AND VISION

VISION

We aim to develop an ecosystem for the youth to provide them with skills, resources and motivation to achieve sustainable development goals.

MISSION

We exist to improve the social and economic condition in and around Kharagpur and inspire and guide the youth to replicate our learnings across the nation.

Accordion

No. Prayas works in three different streams:

  1. Expanding the reach of existing businesses
  2. Helping people,especially rural people,to set up their own business
  3. In-house business ideas by the team

We’d be happy to extend our collaboration to every corner of India. However, to get registered under PRAYAS, the business needs to satisfy the standard guidelines of PRAYAS, following which an MoU will be signed by both the parties.

Mail us at prayas.iitkgp@gmail.com with the objective and current status of your business / startup. Visit https://prayas.gyws.org to know more.

These platforms are generally focussed on providing the members-only with a Platform whereas we aim to deliver both the Platform as well as necessary skills and mindset to the people who can solve social problems whenever they come across one.
We also provide them opportunities in the decision making part, which is certainly missing in the above platforms.People can take up their own initiatives once they are mature enough and work with us to enhance its impact at different places across the Indian continent.

By autonomous we mean we will help students to build a team with a working structure at their LiGHT center. Our aim is to prepare them for the time when they can carry out their action plans and help us with extending our reach to their areas. You will always be a part of our network and take help from us whenever required.
Once you will be mature enough you can help us to expand further and be mentors for several other centers in your locality helping them to be autonomous.

At the beginning of every session, we have a discussion with the centers and have a center research drive in which we discuss the current scenario there- as the members of the centers are the residence so they will have a better understanding of the situation. Then,we select 2 to 3 objectives and have a voting option with all the members and ask them to prepare a list of activities in brief under that social cause which they want to opt for the running year, thereby choosing the objective for the running year.

TIMELINE

Horizontal Timeline →

  1. 2002 Established in 2002 with a mission to improve the living standards of the people of Gopali like villages.
  2. 2004 Registered under the West Bengal Societies Registration Act of 1961.
  3. 2008 Embarked in an education program Jagriti Vidya Mandir in a rented building, focused upon providing quality education.
  4. 2012 2 acres of land was purchased, and the foundation of Jagriti Vidya Mandir was laid.
  5. 2017 GYWS came up with the initiative named LiGHT to reach masses all over India, to reciprocate similar models of social upliftment throughout the nation.
  6. 2018 Started PRAYAS, Business Development Initiative, to work on Rural Entrepreneurship Development and Social Enterprises to help transform lives
  7. 2020 Hostel Construction to provide learning environment to deprived children, begin in Jagriti Vidya Mandir
